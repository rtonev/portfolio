Course Wiz
  • Date: September 2016 - Present
  • Role: UX Researcher/Designer
  • Work Setting: Individual
Summary

Course Wiz is a tool that helps UMSI students pick the right classes during registration. It strives to mitigate any anxiety and stress that a student might have during the process. The tool addresses the problems related lack of peer support, incomplete faculty profiles and missing course data.

Problem Definition

Students have to register for classes each semester. Being able to select the right classes is very important to students and is crucial to their academic success. However, the course selection process could be a difficult and stressful process. There are many classes offered by UMSI, permitting students the valuable freedom of choice. Yet, too many choices also makes it harder for a student to compile their schedule because they must consider a multitude of options and outcomes, while in some cases knowing little details about specific courses. I want to address these issues by developing a web application that gives UMSI students insights into crucial decision-making course details and background information that would help them pick appropriate courses with less efforts.

Discovery

The discovery process involved talking to students in order to understand problems and concerns that they might have. Students told me that they have issues while trying to register for classes.

Competitive Analysis

Once the problem definition was formed and the discovery phase was done, I conducted a competitive analysis, in order see if there are effective tools already addressing this issue. I identified the following competitors and alternatives to my potential solution: Koofers, MyEdu, RateMyProfessors and the UMSI Academic Advising.


Personas

As a result of my conversations and research, I was able to develop target personas.

Brainstorming

I started brainstorming ideas in search of possible solutions. I started sketching possible approaches. Once I had my sketches, I started talking to stakeholders and showed them my sketches. The results from this activity helped me verify ideas and debunk wrong assumptions.

Storyboarding

After getting valuable feedback, I developed storyboards out of my initial sketches.

Interviewing

I met with users once again and this time showed them my storyboards. I also interviewed them in a semi-structured manner. Some of the questions from my interview protocol are listed below:

  • Do you mind if I make a recording in case I miss something?
  • Which courses are you taking this semester?
  • Can you quickly walk me through the process that you went through when picking which classes to take this semester?
    • Did you talk to advising?
    • Looking at tracking and planning sheet?
    • Did you meet academic advising?
    • Did you talk to other students?
  • Are you interested to know what other students are taking?
    • Are you comfortable other students seeing what courses you are taking? Exact Section?
    • Looking at tracking and planning sheet?
    • Would you provide your grades to app admins so that it could be used for showing class grade averages?
  • Before signing up for a class, do you do any lookup of the professors?
    • What information do you think might be valuable to you?
  • What about class average grades or student dropping the class ratio?
Next Steps

I am currently in the process of creating a QOC diagram, which I would follow with prototyping. The prototypes will be shown to users, and after possible (or probable) refining, I would proceed with development.

Iorio's Gelato
  • Date: September 2016 - Present
  • Role: UX Research Consultant
  • Work Setting: Team of 4
Summary

Iorio’s gelato makes authentic Italian gelato. They strive to make a genuine Italian gelato experience that includes the taste of the product, the design of the shop and the interaction between clients and staff. They want to make their customer’s lives more culturally rich by delivering the authentic Italian experience right in their town. They employ a fresh, modern approach to recreate the real Italian gelato. They utilize novel tools and knowledge, combined with using only real, natural and inspired by Italy ingredients. The goal of this project is to produce a recommendations report that the store owners could utilize to improve the customer experience.

Problem Definition

Iorio’s wants our team to find ways in which the customer service flow could be improved. They care about every step in the process, starting from when a customer walks into the store, until the time they leave. Aspects of particular importance include the process to get a gelato, the waiting, tasting and checkout. They want the whole experience to be aligned with and authentically inspired by the Italian mission of Iorio’s. They are not only interested in receiving good service flow tips, but also would like to see a spotlight on potential weaknesses in their process with respect to their Italian mission. They want us to look at both their locations in Ann Arbor, as well as in East Lansing. Another point of interest Iorio’s has is getting recommendations on how to preserve the Italian experience when expanding to potential new locations.

Discovery

In order to get insight into Iorio's business and brand, we explored their Ann Arbor location and reviewed their website. We also conducted a conversation with their owner, which provided us with information about their history, current state and vision for the future.

Background Research

In order to understand Iorio's business better, each team member wrote a background research report. We focused on the following areas:

  • Frozen Dessert Industry
  • Gelato Industry
  • Local Competition
  • Authentic Italian Experience

The report that I was responsible for was about the frozen dessert industry. The reports gave us insights into trends in the industry. We wanted to get a good understanding of the relevant factors that might affect Iorios' performance.

Interviewing

As part of our contextual inquiry, we conducted seven interviews with the various stakeholders at Iorio's, including:

  • Front line staff
  • Management
  • Owners
  • Customers

Below is one of the interview protocols that we used. The protocol shown is for an interview with the front line staff.

Working at Iorio's Gelato
  • How do you like working here?
    • How long have you worked for Iorio’s Gelato
    • Can you think of a something recent that happened at your job, that you liked?
    • How often do things like that happen?
    • Would you like if your manager promoted such initiatives
  • Can you walk me through through a recent work experience that you had, that you did NOT like?
    • How was this handled?
    • Do you think that this could have been prevented?
    • Would you like management to step up more to prevent that in the future?
  • What do you think about the Italian heritage culture of the store?
    • Could you ‘paint a visual picture’ of what an authentic Italian experience is?
    • Could you compare that picture to the the store as it currently is?
    • Do you have any ideas on how to make it a more authentic experience?
  • What do you know about Iorio’s mission?
Training
  • What kind of training did you go through when you started?
    • Who trained you?
    • How long did the training last?
    • What was involved in training?
    • Has there been any additional training since then?
  • Did anything stick out to you from when you went through training?
    • What was really memorable?
  • Is there anything you wish you had learned or think should have been included?
    • Anything that was unnecessary?
Customer Service Flow
  • Think back to the most recent customer you served. Describe to me or walk-through/show me what happened from the moment they walked into Iorio’s to the moment they departed.
    • Be sure to stop interviewee and go back if they aren’t providing the right level of detail.
    • How many customers entered the shop?
    • How were they greeted?
    • What happened next?
    • Describe the steps for fulfilling the order(s)
    • What are the payment options? How is this handled?
    • What else happens before the customers leave?
  • Are some times of the day busier than others?
    • What do you consider a busy time? (how many customers?)
    • Think about the last time the store was busy, how did you handle it?
  • Can you think of a time recently when a customer was unhappy?
    • How was that handled?
    • Does that happen often?
  • How would you rate the customer experience?
    • Have these ratings been measured quantitatively before?
    • Are there things that work well?
    • Are there areas you think could be improved?
    • Anything you think should change?
Product Knowledge
  • How are new menu items/gelato flavors introduced?
    • Sampled by staff?
    • Recipe/ingredient/flavor information from gelato maker?
  • How are flavors chosen for each day?
    • What’s available? Certain ratio of non-dairy/dairy? Certain number of “Italian” flavors (Zuppa Inglese, stracciatella, etc?) Prescribed layout in case?
  • Who chooses the recommended gelato flavors/drink of the month? How frequently do they change?

Affinity Wall

The data that we gathered during the interviews was synthesizes and analyzes by building an affinity wall.

Next Steps

Going forward, we will prepare a recommendations report and present it to our client.

Vehicle and Service Sharing System
  • Date: September 2016 - Present
  • Role: UX Designer | Web Developer
  • Work Setting: Team of 5
Summary

The purpose of this project is to investigate the feasibility, challenges, and opportunities to serve the underserved communities through shared connected vehicles with emerging information and communication technologies. We are studying two types of vehicle-and-service-sharing (V&SS) schemes which we refer as centralized and decentralized systems, and their benefits to underserved communities. The initial focus of the project is to pursue the centralized model.

Problem Definition

People living in underprivileged communities face transportation challenges. In such areas, public transportation is not available or unreliable. Vehicle-sharing services like Zipcar or Uber have open doors for great innovation in transportation via having people share vehicles and thus enable make transportation available to people without vehicles. However, such services have not spread out to underserved communities. We are investigating this problem and will propose a possible solution.

Discovery

In order to figure out details about out target audience, we looked at published literature on the subject, as well as data from Uber rides in the Detroit area. We looked into what the needs of the people in the underprivileged areas around Detroit are. Focus was given on which cities have poor access to public transportation and what are the common needs that people have.

Competitive Analysis

In order to get an understanding of the interactions and workflows involved in a ride-sharing application, I conduced a competitive analysis with popular centralized systems.

Personas

From the data that we gathered, we were able to produce personas that would represent our target audience. Additionally, we developed an anti-persona to help us stay on course with our design and not go off track.


Next Steps

Our next steps are to interview stakeholders and gather more data. Additionally, we will be meeting with employees of community service centers in the area to gain more insights about general ternds and issues that our target population might have. The collected data will drive our prototypes and would help refine our personas and design ideas.
